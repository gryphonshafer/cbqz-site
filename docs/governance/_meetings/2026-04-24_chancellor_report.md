### Commentary

#### Growth

The most exciting news continues to be in the area of growth, both in terms of new team organizations in existing regions and in terms of entirely new regions.

I’m expecting growth in all 3 existing regions, currently anticipating between 2 and 5 new team organizations in Western Washington and between 1 and 2 new team organizations in Inland Northwest (with additional growth opportunities anticipated over the summer in both regions). After this season’s Interregional Championships meet in early May, I plan to restart evangelism and planting efforts in the Oregon region. We made good initial contacts therein last summer, and we added 1 new team organization (though unfortunately only for 1 meet this season). My goal will be to plant enough team organizations in the central and/or southern half of Oregon such that Oregon will reach critical minimum mass to function as a region, which will encourage more growth thereafter due to dramatically reduced meet commute requirements.

Over the past few months, I’ve been actively working on evangelizing and planting 3 new regions: Southwestern Great Lakes, Las Vegas, and Northern California. Each of these are independent efforts with their own in-theater evangelizing and planting team. Southwestern Great Lakes is actively working on recruiting from within a radius of a 2-hour-ish drive, which includes South Bend, Indiana and Grand Rapids, Michigan, and many other surrounding communities. The evangelizing and planting team there has a goal of planting between 10 and 15 team organizations before the autumn. The Las Vegas evangelizing and planting team is actively engaging contacts from around the city and surrounding area with the goal of 5 or more team organizations by the autumn. The Northern California group is working on building their evangelizing and planting team, and they’ll be proceeding initially with evangelism in Redding and surrounding areas.

#### Operations

While our past and anticipated future growth is a wonderful blessing, it can cause strain across a few fronts when it ramps up quickly. It’s trivial to scale QuizSage, and it’s easy and inexpensive to scale the CBQz.org suite of technologies (i.e. regional content management system, season schedule, meet registration, etc.). However, there are 2 areas where scaling remains a challenge (so far):

- Training and mentoring new coaches and magistrates
- LM fabrication and building

Whenever we plant new team organizations ex nihilo, the coaches thereof usually need some level of training and mentoring to quickly and efficiently optimize for effectiveness. Handling that manually is taxing. To help scale this better, I’ve worked and continue to work to create training and instructional videos. But over the summer, I’ll be scaffolding a coach and magistrate mentorship system, wherein new coaches and magistrate candidates can request being paired with an experienced counterpart.

And to scale LM fabrication and building, I’ve been working to establish multiple LM fabrication and build locations. To date, there’s really only been 1 person fabricating and building LMs at scale, Jonathan, the inventor of the LM. In the last few months, Jonathan has updated the LM GitHub repository and has recorded several LM fabricating and building videos. There’s an LM fabricating and building team being organized in the Southwestern Great Lakes proto-region with the intent to build LMs either from pre-fabricated parts or possibly eventually entirely on their own.

If anyone might be interested in participating (part-time and/or temporarily) as either a coach mentor, ministration mentor, LM parts fabricator (the 3D printing part, mostly), or LM builder (the soldering part, mostly), please send me an email: [g@cbqz.org](mailto:g@cbqz.org)

Over the past 3 years, a lot of my focus has necessarily been directed toward my role as Western Washington Regional Coordinator. This was required to sustain Quizzing operations and logistics, including things like stepping in to host-manage some meets. However, as CBQ continues to expand, especially across the continent, it’s becoming increasingly imperative that I need to devote more focus to my role as Chancellor, which requires I step away from regional coordination roles. To that end, I’m excited to hand-off the Regional Coordinator role for Western Washington to Andrew at the season Interregional Championships meet in early May.

#### International Open Championships 2027

To offer additional focus on managing growth, we skipped hosting an International Open Championships (IOC) meet this and last season. We were able to get away with this mostly because all operating regions in those 2 seasons were within driving distance from each other (though a very long drive for some). However, as we add regions that are geographically dispersed across the continent, that will no longer remain true. And so a meet like IOC becomes more critical. It becomes the opportunity each season for all regions to participate in a meet together.

Therefore, I’m planning for a IOC 2027 meet. Exact location and dates are to be determined, but I would expect the 2027 IOC to be held in Western Washington not terribly far from SeaTac airport. It’ll be held likely at the very end of June or very early July, ideally after school is out for most/all and at a time that doesn’t conflict with other Quizzing events. Like IOCs of the past, IOC 2027 will be “open” in that anyone may register to participate regardless of their involvement with CBQ for the season. So we’ll again invite our brothers and sisters from Nazarene, CMA, World, and other Quizzing groups.

#### Finances

Financially, the CBQ business entity continues to be healthy and continues to subsist entirely on voluntary, unsolicited donations. The finance committee regularly reviews and maintains CBQ business entity financials. The CBQ business entity has no regular expenses, and irregular expenses continue to be minimal apart from “Lesser Magistrate” (LM) light/trigger set funding for new team organizations. (Each new team organization is eligible to receive 1 LM system with costs covered by the CBQ business entity, which are under $100 per LM, excluding shipping.) As a result of this, there is currently no annual budget, since it’s nearly impossible to predict revenue and expenses.

Looking out several years into the future, I expect the CBQ business entity will eventually need to transition ask for voluntary donations. The CBQ business entity is a registered 501(c)3 entity recognized by the United States Internal Revenue Service, so all donations are tax deductible. However, to date, the CBQ business entity hasn’t solicited or sought donations. Eventually, this will need to change, though I expect that to not be necessary for some years. However, I’m on record as steadfastly insisting the CBQ business entity never require fees or charge for products or services in excess of costs. In other words, when the CBQ business entity sponsors a meet (versus a region sponsoring a meet), it should not charge more to participate than it expects in costs. Secondary LM prices should always remain close to if not slightly below cost. Over the long-term then, the CBQ business entity can thus only remain financially viable if people believe there’s enough value in it continuing to exist that they voluntarily donate funding.

To that end, I expect it might become good (in a few years) to formerly ask regions after they become incorporated to consider an annual donation of approximately 10% of their annual budget and to remind their members of the opportunity to donate to both the CBQ business entity and their local region. (Regions exist in 1 of 3 states, the last of which is “incorporated” as a 501(c)3 entity. For more details, see [the CBQ regional organizations governance page](https://cbqz.org/governance/regions.md).) However, I maintain strong feelings this should always be entirely voluntary; the CBQ business entity should always be required to perpetually prove its value.

#### Technology

Both QuizSage and the CBQz.org suite of Quizzing tools have matured over the past year. I won’t detail each advancement, because that would be many pages of detail. Instead, I’ll just highlight some of the major developments.

Material labels (predominately in QuizSage) were expanded and extended into a “version 2” that provides significantly simplified advanced functionality like verse additions, blocks, nesting, and distributives. Distributives in particular are especially powerful for constructing varying slopes of verse selection probability in meets relative to Club Lists whilst keeping the material label simple and readable. You can read more about that here:

[https://quizsage.org/docs/material_labels.md](https://quizsage.org/docs/material_labels.md)

I also constructed a translation integration details report as a quick reference for what verses exist or don’t exist across supported translations. For example, some translations include John 5:4 but most don’t. And the block of verses of John 7:53-8:11 are sometimes included and other times not across supported translations. Note also that across translations, some merge verses or split verses and even inverse the content of verses. It gets complicated and messy. QuizSage had always automatically handled excluding verses that don’t appear in all translations included in a given material label; however, this was done silently, so it was easy to miss a verse being excluded. So this report provides transparency into these details. You can read more here:

[https://quizsage.org/docs/translation_integration.md](https://quizsage.org/docs/translation_integration.md)

The CBQ regional content management system and associated season schedule and meet registration system will be opened up for regional self-management likely in June (though possibly July at the latest). Once that’s done, I’ll be reaching out to coordinators of each region to help them get trained and capable of self-managing their content and schedule. Each region can then push content and schedule changes whenever they wish without having to wait for me. That said, I’m happy to continue to assist on request.
